Introduction to Bayesian Optimization

Bayesian optimization (BO) is a technique for optimizing expensive, black-box functions using probabilistic models. It balances exploration and exploitation to efficiently find global optima. BO is particularly useful in scenarios where function evaluations are costly, offering efficiency, flexibility, and uncertainty quantification.

Mathematical Foundation

BO models the objective function as a Gaussian Process (GP), updating it based on observations. Given data, the posterior distribution over the function is defined by the predictive mean and variance. This probabilistic approach allows BO to intelligently search the parameter space for optimal solutions.
