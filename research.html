About My Research

I work with Cornell's Scientific Computing Group doing research in Bayesian optimization. Bayesian optimization (BO) is a technique for optimizing expensive, black-box functions using probabilistic models. It balances exploration and exploitation to efficiently find global optima. BO is particularly useful in scenarios where function evaluations are costly, offering efficiency, flexibility, and uncertainty quantification. As a machine learning researcher, I build foundational knowledge, rooted in mathematics, and I also explore applications of various optimization frameworks. This past semester, I began an in-depth discovery on multi-fidelity Bayesian optimization. I curated a detailed write-up of all I explored and possible research directions for next semester. I also created a BO framework in Julia that you should take a look at. Take a look at these resources if you want more information.
